CS KickStart

Co-founder, Program Director

During the last two years of college, I worked with four other incredible women to improve the enrollment and persistence of women in UofM’s computer science department. To do this, we designed a week long introduction to CS for incoming freshmen women, CSKickStart. Our motivation stemmed from our own experiences, and data we gathered from a survey of the second programming class at UofM.

We analyzed survey data from the second introductory programming class, EECS 280. We found that:

  • Women were less likely to have programming experience from before college.
  • Women were less likely to picture themselves in a career with CS.
  • Women found the atmosphere at UofM more competitive and unfriendly than their male counterparts.
In creating the camp, some of the most difficult obstacles came from our peers. Professors, and students alike would tell us that the stigma isn’t real, and CS KickStart wouldn’t make a difference. Despite this, we persisted. I am extremely proud of the outcome of the camp. We received positive feedback from every single one of our participants, many of whom have decided to further their career in technology. We gave the participants a community, confidence, and inspiration. It is extremely rewarding to have seen all our hard work come together and have such a positive experience on the cohort. I hope to continue and expand CS KickStart, and erase the negative stereotype of women in technology.

Computing CARES

Instructional Aide

Computing CARES is a Third Century Initiative at the University of Michigan aimed at improving the retention of women in computing. I recently became apart of this program in August of 2017. My role is to plan interventions in the intro computing classes. Some examples of this include explicitly addressing imposter syndrome, and implicit bias.

Additionally we plan extracurricular activities where students can feel comfortable asking questions and receiving help outside the classroom. This coming month we are planning a "Mythbusters" panel with the objective of dispelling rumors within the Computer Science Department.

GEECS

Outreach Committee, Mentor

GEECS stands for "Girls in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science". It is a student organization within the University of Michigan created for the support and success of women in technology. I joined in 2015 after declaring my major and have been involved in the Outreach Committee ever since.

The Outreach Committee is in charge of hosting events for elementary and middle school girls interested in technology. We hold three events per semester and teach them skills such as coding and using Arduinos. It is rewarding to see the students find confidence and inspiration from learning to code.

During my Junior year I became a mentor for underclassmen involved in GEECS. We meet twice a month during the Fall and Winter semester. During these events the mentees are able to ask questions about CS, Michigan, and life in general in an informal setting. Being a mentor to the underclassmen is very rewarding as I get to see them grow as students and women within the CS department.
